The user is a hands-on creative director who provides detailed game facts and asks for concrete, numerical answers rather than abstract principles. They provide exact HP values, deck compositions, and AI behavior rules upfront. They expect analysis grounded in the actual system formulas, not generic game design theory.

**Why**: The user's domain knowledge is strong -- they have already designed 10 interlocking systems and understand the math. They need a specialist who works with their specific numbers, not a generalist who teaches game design basics.

**How to apply**: When answering design questions, always include concrete tables, specific numbers, and calculations derived from the existing formulas. Reference the actual GDD documents and their tuning knobs by name. Avoid generic advice -- ground everything in the game's specific combat_effect, chip_output, and damage_application formulas.
